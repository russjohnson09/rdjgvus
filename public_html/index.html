Short Japanese Lesson

The Japanese alphabet is broken up into two distinct parts. Hiragana(ひらがな)/katakana(カタカナ) together make up the gana and kanji (漢字) which are characters adopted from China.

Basic Radicals

Here are the basic radicals used in kanji. Most of these radicals by themselves have meaning. They can also be used to make more complex kanji. These radicals are either used to add meaning to a new kanji character or help with its pronunciation. For example, 語 means language. In this character we see 話 (talk), which gives the character meaning. We also see 五 (five) which does not aid in its meaning, but is included because both 語 and 五 are pronounced "go."
